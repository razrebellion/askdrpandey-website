High-Risk Pregnancy

Some pregnancies need closer monitoring because of factors such as maternal age, existing medical conditions, or complications that arise along the way. Attentive, individualised care helps keep mother and baby safe.

What makes a pregnancy higher-risk

Factors can include conditions such as high blood pressure or diabetes, previous pregnancy complications, multiple pregnancy, or concerns picked up during scans.

How it is managed

Care typically involves more frequent visits, additional scans and tests, and a clear plan tailored to your situation, coordinated with other specialists where needed.

Reassurance and support

The aim is to identify and address concerns early, and to keep you informed and supported throughout your pregnancy.
